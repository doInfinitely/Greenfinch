# Enrichment pipeline — redesign
**Epic ID:** GF-15 | **Phase:** Phase II: Expanded Pilot | **Priority:** P0 — Critical | **Swim Lane:** Data & Enrichment | **Owner:** Remy | **Build Status:** NEW

**Description:** Ground-up rethink of the enrichment pipeline to achieve higher accuracy at lower cost, designed to scale across geographies. The goal is a system that can reliably find, for any commercial property: the owner, the property manager, relevant decision-maker contacts (name, title, email, phone, LinkedIn), the property website, and org affiliations. Remy owns the architecture and approach — this is not prescriptive on method, but clear on outcomes. Must support adding new markets without significant manual rework per geography and should maximize value of subscriptions we've already paid for: PDL and Findymail. Option to drop these later if we find a better approach. If we choose to include residential enrichment, the process should be much more straightforward. We have owner information from appraisal district records — we should be able to do a single call to PDL or waterfall from PDL to 1 or 2 other providers to get the person's contact information. No need to find PM firms etc. Something we haven't talked about yet is phone numbers. Findymail has a phone endpoint that takes a work email and returns a work phone; it's reasonably effective and, in the Replit version of the app, can be triggered via a button. Another option when we don't have great contact info or are missing a phone: Apollo.io has the best enrichment data, but we have to pay them for each AI call and can't reuse, so it can't be our core business model. However, we could have a 'turbo' version of contact data enrichment that uses Apollo and charges our users extra credits that exceed whatever our cost is through Apollo.. but we would have to cache that data at the user organization level. There are many other third party tools out there — PDL, Leadmagic, Hunter.io, Apollo.io, Crustdata, Enrichlayer, CoreSignal. We have engaged with most of these and landed on PDL and Findymail as the best balance of terms (we can use the results in our app across all users) and data quality. Very open to a new or improved approach here, but want to capture the work that has been done and make sure it's super clear.

**Success Criteria:** Measurably higher accuracy than current pipeline across all data dimensions (owner, PM, contacts). Cost per property supports scaling to 500K+ properties economically. Adding a new county/MSA requires configuration, not code changes. Pipeline produces consistent, structured output that the application can consume without transformation. Remy defines and documents the architecture. Challenge here is understanding accuracy/truth. As an initial approach: Assume LinkedIn is the source of truth for current employment and title; Property websites and property management sites that list relevant contacts are a second high-value source of truth; For testing purposes, we can outsource validation to someone on Upwork and ask that they validate through research, direct phone calls, emails, etc. and document the results for ~100-300 properties and associated contacts; Another potential source is our users.
